O1 — Opposites
Start with the consensus at the end of a cycle. What belief has become too crowded, stale, or overextended?

O2 — Obvious
Identify the obvious problem everyone is ignoring. What tension makes the reversal inevitable?

O3 — Outliers
Find the new idea that emerges from the reversal. It looks insane to consensus, but obvious in hindsight.





For O3, the idea must satisfy three conditions simultaneously:

1. It feels absurd

It violates the current worldview.

People laugh.

"God becoming man?"

"Money without a government?"

"Cars driving themselves?"

"Rockets landing themselves?"

The consensus rejects it.

2. It solves a tension everyone feels

This is the key.

The idea is not random.

It emerges because reality is creating pressure.

Examples:

Jesus

Jews waiting centuries for a Messiah
Roman occupation
Desire for redemption

Bitcoin

Internet becomes global
Commerce becomes digital
Trust in banks declines
Money remains tied to nation states

SpaceX

Humanity wants space exploration
Rockets are too expensive
Disposable rockets make expansion impossible

LLMs

Information explodes
Search becomes insufficient
Humans need a way to interact with knowledge itself

The outlier appears exactly where reality is straining.

3. Once seen, it becomes obvious

This is the strange part.

Every great O3 idea has a "how did we not see this?" quality.

After Bitcoin:

Of course the internet would eventually create money.

After reusable rockets:

Of course you wouldn't throw away the rocket.

After LLMs:

Of course humans would want to talk to computers.

After Christianity:

Of course a universal religion would spread through the Roman Empire.

The idea is outrageous before it happens and obvious after it happens.

This is why O3 is not merely "extreme."

It's:

The obvious solution that appears impossible from within the current worldview.

Or even shorter:

Tomorrow's common sense looks insane today.

Notice how beautifully this connects to the other laws:

O1 — Opposites

What assumption is becoming exhausted?

O2 — Obvious

What tension is building?



O3 — Outliers

What impossible solution resolves the tension?

So the sequence becomes:

Pressure → Saturation → Breakthrough

or

O1 → O2 → O3

The Outlier is not random.

It is the release valve for forces that have been building for years.

That's why the biggest ideas create both reactions simultaneously:

"That's ridiculous."

and

"Wait... that actually makes perfect sense."

Heresy → System → Empire

has a natural narrative arc that most of history follows.

Heresy

A belief that challenges the accepted order.

People reject it.

People mock it.

People fear it.

Examples:

Christianity
Heliocentrism
Capitalism
Bitcoin
Reusable rockets
LLMs

The key question:

What belief is considered absurd today?

System

The heresy becomes organized.

The idea gains a vehicle.

Examples:

Church
Scientific method
Corporation
Blockchain
SpaceX
LLM infrastructure

The key question:

How does the heresy become real?

Empire

The system becomes dominant.

The former heresy becomes the establishment.

Examples:

Christendom
Modern science
Global capitalism
Google
Apple
YouTube
Facebook

The key question:

Who captures the new world?





O3A — The Narrow Path

This is about creation.

The more a new idea departs from consensus, the greater its potential impact.

Not because it's different, but because it opens a new territory.

Examples:

Christianity
Bitcoin
SpaceX
LLMs
Airbnb
Uber

The principle is:

The greatest opportunities lie furthest from the crowd.

Or:

The narrow path leads to the largest destination.

This is what Peter Thiel calls going from 0 to 1.


O3B — New Medium

The idea creates a new channel through which value can flow.

Examples:

Christianity

New medium: the Church

Bitcoin

New medium: blockchain networks

Facebook

New medium: social graphs

YouTube

New medium: online video

Apple

New medium: smartphones

NVIDIA

New medium: accelerated computing / AI infrastructure

OpenAI

New medium: conversational interfaces

This is where the idea becomes more than an idea.

It becomes infrastructure.



O3C — The Power Law

This is about selection.

Once a new territory is opened, one or two winners capture most of the value.

Examples:

Religion

Many sects emerged.

One became the Catholic Church.

Search

Many search engines existed.

One became Google.

Social Media

Many social networks existed.

One became Facebook.

Video

Many video sites existed.

One became YouTube.

Smartphones

Many manufacturers existed.

One became Apple.

AI

Many companies exist.

A handful may capture most of the value.

The principle is:

Value does not distribute evenly.

Or:

The winner takes more than expected.


The pattern is:

New ideas begin as outliers and end as monopolies.
