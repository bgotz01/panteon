O1 — Law of the Obvious

The “obvious” is not the solution.

The obvious is the tension.

In 2008, the obvious problem was:

money printing, institutional distrust, and the failure of the financial system.

But Bitcoin itself was not obvious yet. It was technical, obscure, and hard to find unless you were close to computer science or cryptography.

The better question was:

If the old money system is broken, where is the opposite behavior emerging?

Gold was the obvious old answer, but it failed the behavioral test. Young people were not using gold. They did not care about gold. It was mostly older people buying it.

The other obvious signal was that young people were moving online:

social life online
identity online
gaming online
communities online
coordination online

So the deeper obvious was:

If young people are living on the internet, money will eventually become internet-native too.

That is O1.

Not predicting Bitcoin perfectly in 2009.

But recognizing the obvious direction of human behavior before the mainstream fully understands it.

O1 = identify the obvious tension + the obvious behavioral migration.
